[ Where It Is ]

Hwy 40 + Airport Road, Heber City.

Mountain View Commons is located on U.S. Highway 40 in Heber City, Utah — half a mile east of Hwy 189, at the south end of the Airport Road intersection. The site sits less than two miles from the Heber Valley Airport (FAA: HCR / Russ McDonald Field), the regional general aviation airport that handles aircraft up to large corporate jets including Gulfstreams and Global Express. Heber City is the county seat of Wasatch County, located on the Wasatch Back roughly 50 minutes east of Salt Lake City. The site is positioned at the convergence of three primary corridors: north toward Park City and Interstate 80, west to Sundance and Provo via Hwy 189, and south through the Heber Valley toward Strawberry Reservoir.

[ Heber Valley Airport (HCR) ]

Russ McDonald Field.

The Heber Valley Airport sits at the junction of US-40 and US-189, two miles south of Heber City. It's a public general aviation airport with a 6,900-foot runway capable of handling large corporate jets — Gulfstreams, Global Express, Falcon 7X. The FBO offers full Jet-A and 100LL fueling, hangar storage, and ground services.

[ Around the Valley ]

What's within the corridor.

The Heber Valley sits at the center of the Wasatch Back's recreation network. Skiing, golf, fishing, off-road, and the cultural attractions of Park City all sit inside a 30-minute drive.

01

[ WINTER ]

Three resorts within 25 minutes.

Deer Valley (15 min), Park City Mountain (25 min), Sundance (20 min). With the Deer Valley East Village expansion, the closest base lift is shorter than your drive to most Park City Old Town parking.

02

[ WATER ]

Jordanelle in 15. Deer Creek in 5.

Two state-park reservoirs flank the Heber Valley. Jordanelle for boating, paddling, and the new state park marina. Deer Creek for fishing, sailing, and the Provo River fly water immediately downstream.

03

[ TRAILS ]

Wasatch Mountain. Strawberry. Uintas.

Wasatch Mountain State Park borders the valley to the west. Strawberry Reservoir is 25 minutes south on Hwy 40. The Uinta Mountains — the only major east-west range in the lower 48 — start an hour east. Snowmobile, side-by-side, dirt bike, fly rod.
