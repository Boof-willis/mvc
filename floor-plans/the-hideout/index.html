[ PLAN-03 / HIDEOUT / 1 BEDROOM ]

The
Hideout.

One bedroom up top. A double-office middle floor. A drive-through garage filling the entire ground level — no interior bath cutting into usable square footage. The Hideout is the lowest-friction plan: a solo crash pad above a working studio, above the biggest unobstructed garage in the building.

[ Floor by Floor ]

Maximum garage. Minimum residence.

The Hideout dedicates the full ground floor to vehicles. No interior bath at LV-01 means more usable garage square footage than any other plan.

[ Level Detail ]

What lives where.

01

[ LV-01 / FULL GARAGE ]

No bath. No utility room cutting in.

The Hideout is the only plan that gives you the full ground-floor footprint as garage. 20-foot ceilings, drive-through 16-foot doors, and 1,800 square feet of clear floor. Trailers, snowmobiles, classic cars, fabrication equipment — whatever you're parking, this is the configuration with the most room for it.

02

[ LV-02 / DUAL OFFICE ]

Two work zones. One bath.

The middle floor in The Hideout is configured as two adjacent office spaces with a shared bathroom. Use one for client work and one for personal projects. Or build it out as a single open studio with the bathroom serving both ends. Maintains separation from the residential floor above.

03

[ LV-03 / RESIDENCE ]

One bed. Real kitchen.

A single bedroom with a full bath plus the kitchen, dining, and living space all on the same floor. Sized like a refined New York-style loft — efficient, well-appointed, no wasted square footage. This is the Hideout's whole pitch: quiet up top, busy below.

[ Who Buys The Hideout ]

Single owners. Studio buyers. Solo operators.

The Hideout is the lightest residential footprint — and the heaviest-duty garage. Buyers tend to be one of these three.

01

Solo crash pad

The frequent visitor

A pilot, a single founder, a Wasatch Back regular. Not bringing a family. Wants the upstairs to function as a refined one-bedroom with a real kitchen. Comes mostly for ski weekends, summer trail riding, and quick weeknight stays.

02

Studio / workshop owner

Builder, restorer, fabricator

A car restorer, a furniture maker, a fabricator who needs the full ground floor as workshop. The double-office middle floor doubles as a finishing room and customer space. Sleep above your work — but separated by two floors.
